\textbf{Category} & \textbf{Classification criteria} & \textbf{Meaning} \\

Consistent influence
  & $s_c > 0.30$
    \;\textsc{or}\; $z_\mathrm{mid} > 0.15$
    \;\textsc{or}\; ($z_\mathrm{opp} > 0.5$ \;\textsc{and}\;
    ($z_\mathrm{early} > 0.4$ \;\textsc{or}\; $z_\mathrm{mid} > 0.4$))
  & Cadence moved in the target direction throughout the trial,
    including partial responses below the unambiguous threshold
    (compliance score $> 0.30$ or plateau tracking $> 0.15\,\text{SD}$). \\

Impact only from drift phases
  & ($z_\mathrm{early} > 0.15$ \;\textsc{and}\; $z_\mathrm{drop} > 0.15$)
    \;\textsc{or}\;
    ($z_\mathrm{opp} > 0.2$ \;\textsc{and}\; $|z_\mathrm{mid}| < 0.4$
    \;\textsc{and}\; $z_\mathrm{early} < 0.4$)
  & Cadence responded to the ramp-in or ramp-out transitions but not
    the sustained plateau, including weak responses;
    indicates sensitivity to rate of change rather than level. \\

Subconscious correction
  & $z_\mathrm{ci} > 0.5$ \;\textsc{and}\; $|z_\mathrm{early}| < 0.5$
    \;\textsc{and}\; $n_\times \geq 1$
  & Cadence initially followed the manipulation then actively returned
    toward baseline before the trial ended. \\

No clear effect
  & None of the above
  & No systematic directional response in any phase;
    cadence fluctuations within natural variability. \\

\bottomrule

\caption{Behavioural response categories and classification criteria
         ($n = 23$ tempo sessions).
         Categories are evaluated in the order shown; the first match is assigned.
         \textit{Consistent influence} and \textit{Impact only from drift phases}
         each include both unambiguous and partial responses, yielding counts of
         8, 8, 1, and 6 respectively.
         All $z$-scores are in units of each participant's natural cadence
         standard deviation measured during the weight condition.
         $z_\mathrm{drop} = z_\mathrm{early} - z_\mathrm{mid}$;
         $n_\times$ denotes zero-crossings of the deviation-from-target series;
         $s_c$ is the compliance score ($0 =$ unaffected, $1 =$ on target).}
